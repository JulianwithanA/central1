Make a Payment

The Make a Payment screen includes various contextual notifications when applicable, as well as a link to set up automatic payments if the borrower is not already enrolled. The screen also has customized scenarios for the following account statuses: The screen displays custom information for the following scenarios.

  • Account is current and has no past-due payments, fees, or unapplied credits

  • Account is current and can be configured to include making future payments, additional principal/ additional escrow payments, principal only payments, and/ or partial payments)

  • Account is current with unapplied funds and/ or outstanding fees

  • Account has outstanding payments with may or may not include plus delinquency fees

  • Account has an escrow shortage which has the ability to be included with the monthly payment or pay by itself. with monthly payments

  • Account has an escrow shortage only

  • Account has automatic payment active, but the borrower is making another payment

  • Account has an alternate payment plan

  • Any combination of these scenarios

Escrow Shortage Feature

This is a feature in that allows the consumer to make Escrow Shortages payments on their account. The client can require the full amount to be paid or a partial amount to be paid. The client determines what the allowable partial payment will be. The consumer uses the one-time payment screen to make the payment. The amount Escrow Shortage amount is shown below the total due amount. Once that escrow shortage payment is scheduled, the funds will go into the escrow balance field. The funds will be distributed accordingly in LoanServ, and the escrow shortage balance will update if the escrow shortage is not paid in full.

It can take around 24 hours for the balance to reflect since the scheduled payment will need to run on LoanServ's nightly batch cycle.

It is possible that depending on account activity, the payment may not decrease by the entire amount, or another shortage could take place, which could increase the consumer's payment.

This is a feature within CARE that will allow the consumer to schedule a promise to pay from within the Make a Payment screen. A button will be present on the screen that the consumer will click on to schedule the promise to pay. The promise to pay button will appear under the following conditions:

  • The loan is active – (close code = 1 or 9)

  • Number of days delinquent is less than or equal to 30

  • Bankruptcy flag is not equal to ‘A’

  • Promise date exists test [(current calendar date] minus [promise date] is greater than the value inputted into the new system environment setting PTPMaxPromiseDate

When the consumer clicks on the button, then a pop up window will appear. Trigger Recast CIT on OTP Screen

This is a feature within CARE when a large principal curtailment is made on the Make a Payment screen, a pop message appears asking the consumer if they are interested in recast. If the consumer clicks Yes, then a CIT will be created on LoanServ. If the consumer clicks No, then nothing will happen.

Criteria for Triggering Recast CIT on OTP Screen

A loan is eligible for a recast only if the following conditions are met.

  • The close code (PFID 0115417) equals ‘1’ or ‘9’

  • The number of days past due (PFID 0184424) is less than ‘30’

  • Late charge balance (PFID 0121459) equals ‘0.00’

  • Fee balance (PFID 0173329) equals ‘0.00’

  • P&I Advance (PFID 0116157) equals ‘0.00’

  • ARM Flag (PFID 0116153) not equal to ‘1’

  • Loan Type (PFID 0115427 not equal to ‘2’ or ‘3’ or ‘4’ or ‘9’

  • Investor Mnemonic (PFID 0081342) is equal to FNMA or FHLMC.

  • The value in User field 52 (PFID 0087892) is not in the new category within lists

    • The client will be responsible for entering the values with the list.

    • The category is not required so if a client does not use this field, then this condition is not used.

  • The difference between the current calendar date and the last recast date is greater than the value in the environment setting of (RecastNextRecastEligible).
